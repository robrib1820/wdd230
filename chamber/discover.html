The Igapó Lake

Lake Igapó was inaugurated on December 10, 1959, the day of Londrina's Silver Jubilee. It is a place of leisure that also provides the practice of water sports. It has a vast urbanized area with swimming pools, model airplane tracks and a photo club.

The maximum depth of the Igapó lake is only 4.9 meters, and in some places it is no more than 15 centimeters.

Alligators measuring one and a half meters in length live in a tributary of the Igapó lake.

Botanical Garden

The Botanical Garden is considered one of the tourist attractions in Londrina and has an extension that covers more than 1 million square meters of native forest, springs and rivers.The park, opened in 2006, was created for contemplation, reflection and socio-environmental education. Currently, it is considered one of the most important parks for environmental preservation, research and conservation of exotic and endangered species in the State of Paraná.

The Botanical Garden is a free park open to the public with an immense area of Atlantic Forest that is the home to several species of plants and animals. Its mild climate is perfect for walks in the woods.

Strolling through the arboretum, you will find a collection of more than 100 native species of Brazilian flora.

Tomi Nakagawa Square

Tomi Nakagawa Square or Japanese Immigration Square in Londrina, was opened in 2008 to celebrate 100 years of Japanese immigration in Brazil. The inauguration was attended by the Prince of Japan (Naruhito).

Londrina has the 2nd largest Japanese community in Brazil. Tomi Nakagawa was given in honor of the last survivor of the ship that transported the 1st wave of Japanese immigrants to Brazil in 1908 (Kasato Maru).

Next to the monument, a panel contains the names of 104 Japanese immigrants who arrived in Londrina between 1929 and 1934.

ExpoLondrina

Six decades of leading role in national and international agribusiness, and the development of a true ecosystem that combines tradition, innovation, technology, quality information and entertainment.

Considered the largest and most important in the sector, the event reinforces its dynamism annually, and creates favorable environments for new businesses and projects to materialize and rural producers to benefit greatly from new solutions.

ExpoLondrina has become a true national passion. Thousands of people come from the four corners of the country to honor an unforgettable show schedule and a diversified program, focused on gastronomic and cultural experiences.

Londrina Historic Museum

The Historical Museum of Londrina was inaugurated on September 18, 1970, in the former Faculty of Philosophy, Sciences and Letters of Londrina and from 1974 onwards it became a supplementary organ of the State University of Londrina, academically linked to the Center for Letters and Human Sciences.

Since December 10, 1986, it occupies the building of the former Londrina Railway Station, which, due to its architectural lines, constitutes a historic landmark of the English origin of the colonization of Londrina and the New North of Paraná.

In addition to the incredible architecture of the building, you can also find objects and materials that were part of the daily life of Londoners, since the first settlers, there is also a Maria Fumaça that is at the back of the Museum.
